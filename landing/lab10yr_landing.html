The Gap We Fill

Remote sensing sees the surface.
We read what's underneath.

Remote sensing platforms excel at monitoring terrain — LiDAR, hydrology, vegetation indices. Field-scale soil sensors measure physical properties for precision agriculture. Both solve real problems.

Neither can tell you why a specific map unit boundary matters, when a soil survey interpretation is defensible, or where SSURGO's limitations require ground-truth verification.

That knowledge lives with pedologists. We bring it to your decisions.

Our Approach

We know where the data is solid — and where it isn't.

Anyone can pull SSURGO data. The value is in knowing what it means. A soil survey map unit boundary might reflect careful field work — or a surveyor's best estimate from a vehicle in 1974. That distinction changes the interpretation entirely.

Our team brings federal pedologic expertise to every analysis. We combine that domain knowledge with rigorous geospatial modeling to deliver outputs that are accurate, transparent about their limitations, and defensible in front of regulators, investors, and engineers.

No black-box confidence scores. No SoilGrids. Just SSURGO, properly interpreted, spatially modeled, and clearly documented.

"The limitation of a soil survey is known and interpretable by a trained pedologist. That's more valuable than a confident-looking output built on a model nobody can audit."
